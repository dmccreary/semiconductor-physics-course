# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 22 chapters covering all 600 concepts in the learning graph.

## Chapter Overview

1. [Introduction to Semiconductors and Bandgap Fundamentals](01-intro-semiconductors/index.md) — Establishes the materials landscape and the energy band diagram as the central conceptual tool.
2. [Crystal Lattice Structure and Symmetry](02-crystal-lattice-structure/index.md) — Covers real-space crystal structures, reciprocal lattice, Brillouin zones, and diffraction.
3. [Crystal Bonding, Defects, and Surfaces](03-crystal-bonding-defects/index.md) — Examines bonding mechanisms and the full hierarchy of crystal defects.
4. [Quantum Mechanics: Wave Equations and Atomic Structure](04-quantum-mechanics/index.md) — Reviews the Schrödinger equation, potential wells, tunneling, and atomic orbitals.
5. [Bloch's Theorem, Band Formation, and E-k Diagrams](05-bloch-theorem-band-theory/index.md) — Develops band theory from the Kronig-Penney model through effective mass and density of states.
6. [Fermi-Dirac Statistics and Intrinsic Carrier Concentrations](06-fermi-dirac-statistics/index.md) — Derives carrier statistics, intrinsic concentration, and the law of mass action.
7. [Doping, Extrinsic Carriers, and the Fermi Level](07-doping-extrinsic-carriers/index.md) — Covers donor/acceptor doping, ionization regimes, and Fermi-level dependence on temperature and doping.
8. [Carrier Drift, Mobility, and Scattering Mechanisms](08-carrier-drift-mobility/index.md) — Analyzes drift transport, low- and high-field mobility, and all major scattering mechanisms.
9. [Carrier Diffusion, Transport Theory, and Electrical Measurements](09-carrier-diffusion-transport/index.md) — Develops diffusion, the Einstein relation, drift-diffusion model, and Hall effect measurements.
10. [Generation, Recombination, Continuity, and Optical Processes](10-generation-recombination/index.md) — Covers SRH, Auger, and radiative recombination, optical absorption, quasi-Fermi levels, and the continuity equation.
11. [P-N Junction: Equilibrium, Bias, and the Ideal Diode](11-pn-junction-equilibrium/index.md) — Derives the depletion approximation, built-in potential, and the Shockley diode equation.
12. [P-N Junction: Dynamics, Breakdown, and Heterojunctions](12-pn-junction-dynamics/index.md) — Covers breakdown mechanisms, junction capacitance, switching dynamics, and heterojunction band alignment.
13. [Metal-Semiconductor Contacts and MOS Physics](13-metal-semiconductor-mos/index.md) — Analyzes Schottky barriers, ohmic contacts, MOS capacitor regions, threshold voltage, and oxide reliability.
14. [Bipolar Junction Transistors](14-bipolar-transistors/index.md) — Covers BJT operation, current gain, the Ebers-Moll model, Early effect, and high-frequency limits.
15. [JFET, MESFET, and Long-Channel MOSFET Fundamentals](15-jfet-mesfet-mosfet/index.md) — Introduces FET families and develops the long-channel MOSFET I-V model through subthreshold behavior.
16. [Short-Channel Effects, CMOS, and Advanced FET Structures](16-short-channel-cmos/index.md) — Covers DIBL, punch-through, hot carriers, CMOS, and modern FET architectures (FinFET, GAA, SOI).
17. [Optoelectronic Sources: LEDs and Laser Diodes](17-leds-laser-diodes/index.md) — Examines LED efficiency metrics, laser diode operation, and optical cavity structures.
18. [Photodetectors, Solar Cells, and Imaging Devices](18-photodetectors-solar-cells/index.md) — Covers PIN and avalanche photodiodes, solar cell physics, and imaging sensors.
19. [III-V/II-VI Semiconductors, Quantum Nanostructures, and HEMTs](19-compound-semiconductors-quantum/index.md) — Covers compound semiconductors, quantum wells/wires/dots, 2DEG formation, and HEMT devices.
20. [Strained Silicon, 2D Materials, and Power/Microwave Devices](20-strained-si-2d-power/index.md) — Covers strained silicon, graphene, TMDs, carbon nanotubes, and power/microwave device families.
21. [Semiconductor Fabrication Technology](21-fabrication-technology/index.md) — Covers crystal growth, epitaxy, oxidation, diffusion, ion implantation, lithography, etching, and metallization.
22. [Device Characterization and Compact Modeling](22-characterization-modeling/index.md) — Covers electrical and optical characterization techniques, compact models, noise, and tunneling devices.

## How to Use This Textbook

Chapters are ordered to respect concept dependencies: each chapter assumes mastery of all concepts in preceding chapters.
Chapters 1–10 build the quantum mechanical and statistical foundations; Chapters 11–18 cover the major device families;
Chapters 19–22 address advanced materials, fabrication, and measurement.
Students new to quantum mechanics should read Chapters 4–5 carefully before proceeding to device chapters.
