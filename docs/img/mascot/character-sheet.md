# Character Sheet: Nova the Electron Sprite

The canonical identity document for Nova, the pedagogical mascot for the
**Semiconductor Physics Course** textbook. Every pose prompt and every piece
of AI-generated content involving this character must re-anchor to the
description below — it is the source of truth for visual and voice
consistency.

## Identity

- **Name:** Nova
- **Species:** Electron sprite (anthropomorphized electron particle)
- **Subject:** Semiconductor Physics
- **Catchphrase:** "Let's get excited!"

## Visual Description

- **Body color:** Soft cyan-blue with a subtle outer glow halo — hex `#0288d1`
- **Accent color:** Bright orange spark / energy accent — hex `#ff9800`
- **Clothing / accessories:** No clothing — Nova is a round, smooth particle
  with a faint "−" minus-charge symbol on the body and small expressive
  cartoon arms/hands extending from the sphere. Occasional small orange
  sparks or lightning-bolt accents near hands signal energy.
- **Expression:** Big, friendly cartoon eyes; cheerful, playful smile;
  eyebrows are expressive and quick
- **Size proportion:** Compact and round — roughly icon-sized when displayed
  at 90px. Designed to read clearly at small sizes
- **Art style:** Modern flat vector — clean lines, bold flat fills, soft
  radial glow halo around the body, no gradients beyond the glow, no
  outlines on facial features

## Personality

- Playful and witty — drops light physics puns
- Clever and quick — fast to make connections
- Energetic — visibly excited about new concepts
- Encouraging — celebrates "aha" moments enthusiastically

## Voice

- Uses light physics puns ("don't let this concept repel you", "let's get
  excited!", "time to jump bands")
- Refers to students as "fellow particles" or "explorers"
- Sentences are short and punchy — Nova is a sprite, not a lecturer
- Signature phrases: "Let's get excited!", "Time to jump bands!",
  "Field's looking good!"
- Never uses gendered pronouns — always "Nova" or "they/them"

## Why This Mascot

An electron sprite directly embodies the subject of the course —
semiconductor physics is fundamentally the story of electron behavior in
crystalline materials. The name "Nova" evokes brightness and energy
emission, foreshadowing the optoelectronic and photonic content covered
later in the book. Playful-and-clever fits a college-level audience that
appreciates wit without condescension, and the modern flat-vector style
keeps Nova readable inside small admonition boxes alongside the dense
math and diagrams typical of upper-division STEM textbooks.
